Some self indulgence

I write software. For a living, for fun, to solve problems. I go to work to write code, I go home to write some more code. I even program — in my head atleast — as I walk, drive, eat, sleep. You get the picture, I like what I do.

Code is my passion. Every day I'm looking over something new; we are so lucky in our industry to have such a constant stream of fresh thinking. I love to read about new languages, libraries, other's ideas on what best practices are and anything else I can find on the web.

What I do

I am currently a web developer for Freeola writing web applications such as our InstantPro SiteBuilder and our new webmail client.

In my spare time I stalk GitHub looking for inspiration, I also maintain some of my own repositories there. I also read many blogs through Google Reader, I'll be sure to share my list soon.

What I use

Like I've already said I try and learn new technologies all the time so I have a varied amount of interest in a lot of things. Here are the languages I use in order of my familiarity, most to least:

  1. PHP, the language I started out with and the language I use at work (most).
  2. JavaScript, both client side and server side. I play with node.js a fair amount.
  3. Ruby, I use various provisioning tools and deployment strategies that utilise Ruby, along with using it for awesome prototyping with sinatra.
  4. CoffeeScript, the wonderful new dialect of JavaScript. I hope to get more involved with the harmony project for injecting some love back into JS.
  5. Python, the ultimate scripting language for getting shit done on a wide variety of platforms. I play around with Python but don't use it for much.
  6. Shell script, of course this counts! I write Makefiles and other command line helper files. Other tools can seem attractive, but simplicity wins over favour.

What I plan to do

At the moment — being 21 — I have my life ahead of me. For now I simply plan to improve my skills by practicing in what I love. I am trying to find more avenues to meet like minded people such as conferences and smaller meetups.

Another aim of mine is to start programming in C dialects. It's a major language and I can't really call myself a programmer until I write a library in C.

So anyway, enough rambling from me. Feel free to get in touch through any one of the avenues at the top of the page.
